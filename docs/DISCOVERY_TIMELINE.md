# GEO Object Reconstruction Lab — Discovery Timeline

## Stage 0 — Initial Observation

The original question was not cosmological.

The starting observation was:

Newton gravity and Coulomb interaction share a highly similar structure.

This suggested that mass and gravity could potentially be expressed through a richer geometric representation.

The objective was to explore whether observable mass and total gravitational response were necessarily identical quantities.

---

## Stage 1 — Geometric Residual

By expressing the problem geometrically, a residual term appeared.

The system did not converge toward a perfect identity.

Instead, a small geometric discrepancy emerged.

This discrepancy later became the first epsilon-like signature.

---

## Stage 2 — Ring Emergence

Repeated evaluations revealed preferred numerical regions.

The values were not random.

Three recurrent signatures appeared:

- 3/4
- sqrt(3/5)
- pi/4

The persistence of these values suggested the existence of a stable geometric structure.

The concept of a geometric ring emerged.

---

## Stage 3 — Object Recognition

The question changed.

Instead of asking:

"What equation explains the values?"

the question became:

"What object naturally contains these values?"

The search was no longer numerical.

It became geometric.

A single geometric architecture appeared capable of reproducing all observed signatures.

At this point GEO stopped being descriptive and became predictive.

---

## Stage 4 — Emergent Properties

Once the object was identified, new properties emerged naturally:

- coupling
- complementarity
- stable angular regions
- geometric efficiency
- projection behavior

The efficiency parameter later identified as eta emerged from the object rather than being imposed.

---

## Stage 5 — Observer Geometry

The ant journey thought experiment appeared.

The focus shifted from:

"What is the object?"

to:

"What does an observer inside the object perceive?"

This led to:

- folded-space intuition
- support deformation
- projection geometry
- local versus global structure

---

## Stage 6 — Coupling Geometry

The object evolved from:

active / inactive regions

toward:

observable / complementary regions

connected through a coupling structure.

The geometry was reinterpreted as a single coupled system rather than two disconnected systems.

---

## Stage 7 — Derived Laboratories

Multiple studies emerged as consequences of the object:

- SOP investigations
- GEO Math Lab
- Gravity Dynamics
- Vacuum Harmonics
- Hubble reconstruction
- Projection and coherence studies

These laboratories explored different manifestations of the same underlying geometry.

---

## Stage 8 — Reconstruction Lab

This laboratory returns to the origin.

Its purpose is not to validate later consequences.

Its purpose is to reconstruct the object itself from the original signatures.
